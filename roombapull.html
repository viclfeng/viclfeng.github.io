Roomba Pull

iRobot Corporation, Mechanical Engineering Internship, Summer 2018

As an intern at iRobot, all the engineering interns were tasked with a tractor pull style challenge. I worked in a team of 3, including a software/hardware engineering, myself, and another one of the mechanical engineering interns. The constraints placed on this challenge were that we needed to create our own hook attachment and that we could only use standard Roomba wheels. Our team ended up winning the challenge after our Roomba pull 100lbs.

After digging through the discarded Roombas, our team decided that our approach was to simply attach two extra wheel modules to the existing Roomba body and rework the original circuit board to use the signals directed to the blower and cornerbrush to power the wheels. I worked primarily on the wheel module attachment which was modeled in Creo, the faceplate, and machining the hook attachment.
